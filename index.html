About The Team

Molecular diagnostics embedded in real-world infectious disease care.

Our group contributes to molecular diagnostics and genomic surveillance efforts at INBI Matei Bals. We combine laboratory rigor, reproducible pipelines, and cross-disciplinary interpretation to support clinicians, microbiologists, and research collaborators.

We work on both targeted and untargeted strategies, from pathogen detection to variant analysis and microbiome-style profiling, with a practical focus on answer quality and turnaround.

Quality principles

  • Traceable processing from sample intake to report release
  • Standardized controls across molecular workflows
  • Versioned bioinformatics pipelines and documented parameters
  • Clear communication with partner clinical teams

Capabilities

Sequencing and analysis areas

Viral Genomics

Sequencing for surveillance, lineage resolution, mutation profiling, and outbreak support.

Bacterial and Fungal DNA

Genome-level and targeted analyses for identification, characterization, and comparative studies.

Shotgun Metagenomics

Community-level profiling from mixed samples with taxonomic and functional interpretation.

16S rRNA Profiling

Targeted microbial community assessment for ecological and clinically relevant investigations.

WGS Pipelines

Whole-genome sequence analysis including QC, assembly, variant detection, and annotation.

Bioinformatics Reporting

Method-aware outputs tailored for collaborators who need concise and interpretable results.

Sample types we commonly process

  • Clinical specimens from infectious disease investigations
  • Environmental samples for microbial monitoring programs
  • High-complexity mixed microbial communities
  • Samples requiring custom extraction and workflow adaptation

Analysis outputs

  • Taxonomic profiling and relative abundance summaries
  • Assembly and genome quality metrics
  • Variant calls, phylogeny support, and comparative insights
  • Integrative interpretation aligned with project goals

Workflow

From sample to scientific or clinical insight

  1. 1. Intake and triage

    Sample metadata check, feasibility review, and workflow planning.

  2. 2. Wet lab processing

    Extraction, QC, library preparation, and sequencing setup.

  3. 3. Computational analysis

    QC filtering, classification, assembly, and variant-focused workflows.

  4. 4. Interpretation

    Cross-disciplinary review to convert data into usable findings.

  5. 5. Reporting and follow-up

    Clear deliverables, reproducibility notes, and next-step recommendations.
