Research Areas

Lab and computational workflows for infectious disease genomics.

Our work spans sequencing, quality control, comparative analysis, and reproducible reporting across multiple pathogen classes.

Domains

  • Viral, bacterial, and fungal genomics
  • Shotgun metagenomics and 16S profiling
  • WGS assembly, annotation, and variant analysis
  • Phylogenetics and AMR marker analysis
  • Pipeline engineering and reproducibility documentation

Whole Genome Sequencing

Reference-guided and de novo workflows with QC, assembly checks, and annotation.

Metagenomic Profiling

Taxonomic and functional analysis of mixed microbial communities from complex samples.

16S rRNA Analysis

Targeted community profiling with transparent methods and reproducible outputs.

Phylogenetics

Evolutionary and transmission-focused analyses for surveillance and outbreak work.

AMR Profiling

Genomic detection and reporting of antimicrobial resistance determinants.

Pipeline Development

Automated and versioned workflows for multi-sample genomic studies.

Sample Types

  • Clinically relevant infectious disease specimens
  • Environmental microbial monitoring samples
  • High-complexity mixed microbial samples
  • Longitudinal and cross-sectional datasets

Standard Outputs

  • QC metrics and reproducibility notes
  • Taxonomic and genomic summary reports
  • Variant and comparative genomic interpretations
  • Methods-ready text, figures, and tables
